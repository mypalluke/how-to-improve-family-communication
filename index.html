No. 30 How to Improve Family Communication

30 Minute Public Discourse

INTRODUCTION (5 min)

(Walk to the podium looking at your phone. Type a quick message. Pause for 2 beats. Put the phone in your pocket and look up.)

I apologize for that. I was just sending a quick message. (pause 1 beat) But let me ask you... how did that make you feel?

It certainly didn't make you feel connected to me, did it? You probably thought, "This brother has a real problem, and he's going to talk to us about communication?"

It is incredible when you think about it. Never in human history have we had so many tools to stay connected. We can text, video chat, and instantly share photos with people on the other side of the planet. (pause 1 beat) And yet, despite all this amazing technology, there is still a massive problem with communication in the home.

If we have all the tools in the world, what's still missing? The wisdom of the One who actually originated the family arrangement—Jehovah God.

We all yearn to be close to our family members, but communication in today's world is difficult. And a lack of meaningful communication can lead to deep misunderstandings and regrets.

Notice what Proverbs 15:22 says:

Proverbs 15:22
"Plans fail when there is no consultation, But there is accomplishment through many advisers."

This is a scripture that clearly shows cause and effect. "Consultation" is simply another word for communication. So, according to this verse, what happens if there is no communication? (pause 1 beat) Failure!

But if all in the family are working towards communication, what is the result? (pause 1 beat) Accomplishment! Open, respectful, and loving communication is essential for family happiness.

(SHOW ARTWORK: Family not communicating well)

Let’s take a look at this picture. The dad is troubled, and the mom is thinking, “These kids and their devices!! Ugh!!” and the kids are in their own little world. They don't even know their parents are upset.

What advice would you give this family? (pause 1 beat)

Have you ever watched a movie where the protagonist is always getting himself into trouble because he either didn't communicate something simple to someone, or there was a miscommunication that produced a chain of unstoppable events? It can be so frustrating to watch! You want to reach into the screen and shake some sense into them!

Often, the resolution of such stories is clear and open communication. Don’t you want to reach into the screen and shake some sense into this family?

How about your life? Do you feel like your family life sometimes feels like one of those tragic comedies?

(HIDE ARTWORK)

We all live busy lives, our devices are distracting, and none of us are perfect. Each of us has our own quirks that can make communication difficult.

So, how can we improve our communications within the family? Proverbs 24:3 says “by wisdom a house is built up.” Today we are going to see how applying Bible principles—the wisdom from above—will help us improve our communication within the family.

Pay attention to these three key points:

  1. How husbands and wives must work hard to communicate with one another.
  2. How children and parents need to keep the lines of communication open.
  3. And how worshipping Jehovah promotes good family communication.

HUSBANDS AND WIVES (10 min)

So first, let's talk about communication between husbands and wives.

First, it's appropriate to acknowledge that men and women are different. Not just in our physical traits but also in our mental and emotional traits. It’s important to recognize these differences and appreciate them. It is the way Jehovah made us.

Genesis 2:18
"Then Jehovah God said: 'It is not good for the man to continue to be alone. I am going to make a helper for him, as a complement of him.'"

God created the man and woman to complement each other, not to copy each other. This puts our differences into a beautiful Scriptural context.

That being said, we shouldn’t be surprised that men and women communicate differently than one another. Generally, women prefer to talk out a problem before hearing a solution; in fact, sometimes talking is the solution. And men tend to think in terms of solutions.

Marriage unites two minds, two personalities, two backgrounds—even two tongues. That's quite a challenge! But understanding this is essential to a happy marriage.

So what can husbands do? One Bible principle that can help us can be found at 1 Peter 3:7:

1 Peter 3:7
"You husbands, in the same way, continue dwelling with them according to knowledge. Assign them honor as to a weaker vessel, the feminine one, since they are also heirs with you of the undeserved favor of life, in order for your prayers not to be hindered."

"Dwell with them according to knowledge."

The footnote in our study Bible says this means “showing them consideration; understanding them.” In simple terms—a husband needs to understand his wife's needs, not just physical, but emotional, psychological, and spiritual. That's what it means to respect her feminine dignity.

The beautiful thing about how Jehovah created us is that we are all different. Husbands, do you really understand your wife? Do you know what makes her unique? (pause 2 beats)

To illustrate this, we almost all have these amazing devices in our pockets: The smart phone. With a basic understanding of the device you can make a phone call, send a text, take a picture. But if you took the time to study your smart phone you would realize you can do so much more! Some people have recorded and edited entire movies just with a smart phone. The possibilities seem endless.

Husbands, if you take the time to really learn your wife, you'll unlock what Jehovah purposed for husbands and wives. Ecclesiastes 4:9, 10 puts it simply: "Two are better than one... for if one of them falls, the other can help his partner up."

This understanding will go a long way to you fulfilling the next portion of 1 Peter 3:7: “Assign them honor as to a weaker vessel.”

When husbands recognize the amazing and unique role women fulfill in Jehovah’s purpose, they both honor Jehovah and their marriage. Husbands will then treat her as a precious vessel.

Think about this: have you ever known someone that bought a $1000 phone? What is the first thing they do to that phone? They put a protective case on it and they care for it as if it were a baby! But, little by little they forget about that initial investment until one day they accidently drop it and the screen shatters.

Honoring your wife as scripture says takes time and attention—it won't come automatically. The two halves of that verse go hand in hand: knowledge leads to honor.

(SHOW ARTWORK: Husband paying attention to his wife)

Look at how this husband is paying attention to his wife. He's focused. He's listening to her heartfelt concerns. Her opinions may differ from his own—but he values them deeply.

If a husband was to neglect his role in this manner, the last part of the verse shows that even his prayers to God could be hindered. A $1000 smartphone with a broken screen cannot even make a phone call. (pause 2 beats)

So here is the point: If a husband does not follow this counsel, Jehovah will not listen to his prayers.

Yes, it matters to Jehovah how a husband treats his wife. Take the time. Really understand her. Honor her.

So what can wives do to contribute to good communication with their husbands?

Likewise, she too can apply these same points and take the time to better understand her husband. Note what the scriptures say at Proverbs 31:26:

Proverbs 31:26
"She opens her mouth in wisdom; The law of kindness is on her tongue."

This scripture is grouped with a number of other scriptures describing a capable wife. Certainly, all you wives would like to be described by your husbands as being capable! And this scripture shows one of the keys to become such a capable wife.

She opens her mouth in wisdom. Above all, the capable wife is a spiritual person—she fears God and worships him with deep respect. She recognizes that true wisdom comes from above. So when she speaks her mind, it comes out respectful and kind.

Remember the scripture at Proverbs 14:1: "The truly wise woman builds up her house, But the foolish one tears it down with her own hands."

Optional Cut — Layer 1 (~2.7 min)

Consider a situation that arose in the life of Isaac and his wife, Rebekah, as recorded at Genesis 27:46–28:4. Let’s read a portion of that.

Genesis 27:46
"After that Rebekah kept saying to Isaac: 'I am disgusted with my life because of the daughters of Heth. If Jacob ever takes a wife from the daughters of Heth, like these daughters of the land, what good is my life?'"

This example shows that there is nothing wrong with a wife showing or displaying strong feelings on a matter. A wife builds up her house when she takes time to consider how she will express herself and do it with kindness.

Notice how Isaac responds in the next verse: "So Isaac called Jacob and blessed him and commanded him, saying: 'You must not take a wife from the daughters of Canaan.'"

There is no indication that Isaac responded harshly. Instead, what did he do? He sent their son Jacob away to find a God-fearing wife who was not likely to become a source of distress to Rebekah.

Suppose that a disagreement arises between a husband and his wife. A subtle shift from “you” to “I” can prevent a minor disagreement from escalating into a heated argument. For example, instead of saying, “You never spend time with me!” why not say, “I wish we could spend more time together”?

Imagine if Rebekah had said it this way: "You need to go and talk to your son and make sure he doesn’t marry one of those girls he’s been talking to!"

No, she wisely, and thoughtfully explained the situation to Isaac and how it was affecting her. Focus on the problem, not just the person. Resist the tendency to analyze who is right and who is wrong as it says at Romans 14:19: “Pursue the things making for peace and the things that are upbuilding to one another.”

Yes, for both husbands and wives, meaningful heartfelt communication takes time and effort. A question for self-evaluation as a married couple: Do we regularly set aside time to talk together? Do we include Jehovah in our discussions—praying together before we decide?

Do this regularly—before problems ever come up. If you're already communicating well, you'll draw together naturally when trouble does come.

But more importantly, when problems arise, what will help you communicate effectively as a couple? 1 Peter 3:8:

1 Peter 3:8
"Finally, all of you have unity of mind, fellow feeling, brotherly affection, tender compassion, and humility."

All of these traits are important, but humility will stick out as being the most important. All these other things revolve around humility.

Humility will allow you to view your mate as you should. As verse 7 brings out, you both “stand before Jehovah heirs of the undeserved favor of life”. If we are not humble, it will lead to pride and envy.

Work on humility on yourself and realize that you both need to apologize and be forgiven. (pause 2 beats)

PARENTS AND CHILDREN (10 min)

And so, how about communication between parents and children? How can parents and children improve communication?

Let’s talk with the parents first. It is your responsibility to keep the lines of communication open with your children. In order to do this parents need to be approachable and listen patiently to their children.

How? Following this Bible principle can help. James 1:19:

James 1:19
"Know this, my beloved brothers: Everyone must be quick to listen, slow to speak, slow to anger."

Every family member can benefit from following this Bible principle. And it is pretty self explanatory: Quick to listen, slow to speak. (pause 1 beat) Quick to listen, slow to speak.

Optional Cut — Layer 4 (~0.5 min)

After conducting many interviews with young people, the authors of the book For Parents Only noted this barrier to communication. They say: “The kids’ number one most common complaint about their parents was, ‘They don’t listen.’” That problem is hardly one-sided. Parents frequently make the same complaint about their children.

Here's the point: To keep the lines of communication open, family members must listen—really listen—to one another.

Parents, do you really listen to your children? This may be difficult when you are tired or when the conversation seems somewhat trivial. But what appears to be trivial to you may be very important to your child.

Think about the Bible principle found at Luke 16:10: "The person faithful in what is least is faithful also in much, and the person unrighteous in what is least is unrighteous also in much."

Those "little things" your children bring to you now prove that you care. Show interest in the trivial stuff, and when the big issues come, they'll come to you.

That’s what it means to be approachable!

As parents, we need to remember that our children do not have the same experience in life as we do. Everything is fresh to them, you have a unique opportunity and responsibility to help them to grow and learn. You only have one opportunity to do this. (pause 1 beat) DO NOT throw it away.

Open your bibles with me to Ephesians 5:15, 16 (this will really drive home the point of our use of time):

Ephesians 5:15, 16
"So keep strict watch that how you walk is not as unwise but as wise persons, making the best use of your time, because the days are wicked."

Notice the scripture says we need to keep strict watch, to be wise, because the days are wicked. No better time to apply this than right now—we need to stay alert, and we need Jehovah's help to do it.

But he gives us the answer right here in the scripture on how to do this: make the best use of your time.

Optional Cut — Layer 2 (~0.6 min)

Imagine you have been given a valuable inheritance. How would you use it? Pay off your mortgage? Buy a new car? Invest it? Save it? How you would use it would say much about what is important to you.

Parents, your children are actually a valuable inheritance from Jehovah. What are you doing with that inheritance? You have a responsibility greater than any other responsibility you can think of.

It can appear that giving your children the needed time and attention may be something you can put off till another day. After all, you have seemingly urgent matters to attend to right now! But those urgent matters can choke off your time from actual important things in life. What you use your time for will show you what is most important to you.

So if you feel guilty of this, some self-evaluation may be warranted.

⏱ Should have ~11:14 LEFT here — keep if on pace, cut if behind
Optional Cut — Layer 3 (~0.5 min)

There is a useful article in the Watchtower August 1st, 2006: “You cannot save it, so use it well”. It highlights two key points for time management: Avoid procrastination and cut down on clutter.

Do you find yourself constantly juggling your job, home, hobbies, trips, and a host of other things? If so, perhaps it's time to reduce the clutter, spiritually speaking.

These are urgent times. Make the best use of your time—make time for your children.

Children, all these principles that we have talked about so far can also apply to you. Remember this important point: Your parents love you very much and really want what is best for you. The Bible says at Proverbs 1:5, "a wise person listens and takes in more instruction. A man of understanding acquires skillful direction."

10:00 LEFT

Be slow to speak, and think about what your parents are trying to say. They've experienced much of what you're going through, and want to protect you from mistakes—maybe ones they made themselves. View your parents as allies, not adversaries.

Honor your father and your mother was a command that Jehovah gave to the Israelites and something that was passed to the Christians too. This is the first command Jehovah gave that had a promise connected to it. The promise is that things will go well with you and you will live a long time! The hope is that you will be able to live forever! Of course, it is wise to obey your parents as long as what they say doesn’t go against Jehovah.

But sometimes you may feel like you are not being heard. Keep trying, be willing to share your thoughts and your feelings with your parents. Remember that they have a lot going on in their lives and are imperfect.

When you were young, your parents had many rules. One of them may have been “don’t play in the street.” You may have questioned their thinking ability then, because you may have thought “there are no cars in the road!” “They just don’t want me to have fun!”

Even at a young age, you could be reasoned with and could see the principle behind such a rule and you would obey because you knew your parents loved you. By taking the time and paying attention to what your parents said, you are alive today and have benefited from their rules.

When you grow older there are different kinds of dangers. But, like before, your parents' experience and wisdom can help you to avoid many pitfalls and disasters. Likely, you have more freedom now, however, obey the rules your parents still have for you.

Just like before, allow your parents to reason with you. This will take some time, you may not understand fully why they tell you what they tell you, but pay attention to what they say, this will preserve your life! Remember, your parents love you and want what is best for you.

Communication really goes both ways—and both parents and children need a healthy dose of humility to make it work. Philippians 2:3, 4 puts it well:

Philippians 2:3, 4
"Do nothing out of contentiousness or out of egotism, but with humility consider others superior to you, as you look out not only for your own interests, but also for the interests of others."

OK, so we always hear this—don't be contentious, be humble. But here's the real question: next time you catch yourself becoming that way, how do you actually apply this scripture in the moment?

For example, sometimes we might catch ourselves starting to say something that is really upsetting to whomever we are talking to. Right then, we can stop mid-sentence and simply say, "Oof... that didn't come out right. Can I start over?" Then, we can reexamine how we could say it better.

Of course, it would be better not to say something hurtful in the first place, but we are not perfect. Who can completely control his tongue? Are any of us perfect? What scripture supports that? James 3:2 reminds us: "For we all stumble many times. If anyone does not stumble in word, he is a perfect man." Since none of us are perfect, humility allows us to hit the reset button.

Optional Cut — Layer 7 (~0.2 min)

Many times, in driving you need to yield. Yielding to other drivers can sometimes take humility. But, if you don’t yield, eventually, you will crash.

Both parents and young ones need to learn to be humble. Both need to give way to each other and truly listen. If you work together, you will get much accomplished and you will be happy.

When your parents have you do chores, remember—that's an extension of their love. They want you to succeed, and learning to work hard, even when you don't want to, will help you get there. Do it happily, not begrudgingly, and you'll keep those lines of communication open.

When they organize time to worship Jehovah together as a family, take the time and pay attention to what is being taught, and participate to the best of your ability, this will help you see the love that your parents have for you and will build a relationship that will last forever between you, your parents, and Jehovah!

05:00 LEFT
WORSHIPPING JEHOVAH & CONCLUSION (5 min)

Communication within the family leads to happiness now and to God's approval. Jehovah, our Father, loves us deeply and wants us happy and close to him.

James 4:8
"Draw close to God, and he will draw close to you."

As we strengthen our relationship with Jehovah, we will see our relationships with others naturally improve. Even if not all family members worship Jehovah, family communication will benefit from the efforts of those who apply Bible principles.

Of course, if we worship Jehovah together as a family it will strengthen the bond even more. This bond that we have with Jehovah will give us a magnetic personality. A magnet with iron is strong, but a magnet with another magnet is even stronger!

What can you do right now? If you're a family head, take the lead. If you're a family member, cooperate fully.

Remember this: Families that do not spend time together, do not communicate well. Families that do not communicate well, have problems.

This isn't just an opinion; it is a Scriptural reality based on the verses we've discussed today. As we saw in Proverbs 15:22, without consultation, plans fail. But to have that consultation, you must apply Ephesians 5:15, 16 and "make the best use of your time." Good communication doesn't happen by accident; it requires an intentional investment of your time.

So, here are five practical things to work on:

  1. Eat a meal together at least once a day as a habit. Like the early Christians at Acts 2:46, take your meals together with "great rejoicing."
  2. Pray together constantly (1 Thessalonians 5:17). Not just at meal time, but deep meaningful prayer. Family heads, ask for suggestions for things to pray for.
  3. Be together during the meetings. Sit together as a family to apply Hebrews 10:24, 25 and "incite one another to love and fine works."
  4. Play together. Ecclesiastes 3:4 reminds us there is a "time to laugh" and a "time to skip about." Families that play together, stay together!
  5. Study the Bible together at least once a week. Follow Deuteronomy 6:6, 7 to speak of God's words when you "sit in your house." Dig deep and encourage one another.

Earlier, we talked about how people will immediately put a protective case on a new smartphone to protect their investment. (pause 1 beat) These five steps are the protective case for your family.

If you put these into practice, you won't have to worry about missing opportunities to tell your family how much you love them. Applying Bible principles in our lives, especially what it says about families, will improve our communication right now.

Jehovah, the one that all families owe their name to, has given us direction:

For husbands and wives, he requires the husband to be understanding and reasonable and to show his wife honor. And the wife to be wise, respectful, and kind.

For children and parents, he requires the parents to keep the lines of communication open and to be quick to listen and slow to speak. And he requires children to be obedient, and likewise quick to listen and slow to speak.

In the introduction, we talked about those frustrating movies where a simple conversation could have fixed everything. Usually, right at the end, the protagonist finally realizes what needs to be done—and there's a happy resolution.

It would be nice if life was exactly like that—where one big realization fixes everything forever. But in reality, every day we need to be looking for ways to improve ourselves, and communication within the family is no different.

When we worship Jehovah together as a family, and try our very best to do his will and apply his principles to our lives daily, communication will become much easier. It will become as easy as breathing. And you will live a life without regrets.
